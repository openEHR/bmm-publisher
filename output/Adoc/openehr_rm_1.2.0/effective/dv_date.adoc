2+^h|*DV_DATE*

2+a|Represents an absolute point in time, as measured on the Gregorian calendar, and specified only to the day. Semantics defined by ISO 8601. Used for recording dates in real world time. The partial form is used for approximate birth dates, dates of death, etc.

|xref:BASE:foundation_types:time_types.adoc#_time_definitions_class[Time_Definitions].*valid_iso8601_date* ( +
s: `xref:BASE:foundation_types:primitive_types.adoc#_string_class[String][1]` +
): `xref:BASE:foundation_types:primitive_types.adoc#_boolean_class[Boolean]` [1..1]
a|String is a valid ISO 8601 date, i.e. takes the complete form:

* `YYYY-MM-DD` (extended, preferred) or one of the partial forms `YYYY-MM` or `YYYY`
* `YYYYMMDD` (compact) or a partial variant `YYYYMM`.

Where:

* `YYYY` is the string form of any positive number in the range `0000` - `9999` (zero-filled to four digits)
* `MM` is `01` - `12` (zero-filled to two digits)
* `DD` is `01` - `31` (zero-filled to two digits)

The combinations of `YYYY`, `MM`, `DD` numbers must be correct with respect to the Gregorian calendar.

|xref:BASE:foundation_types:time_types.adoc#_time_definitions_class[Time_Definitions].*valid_iso8601_time* ( +
s: `xref:BASE:foundation_types:primitive_types.adoc#_string_class[String][1]` +
): `xref:BASE:foundation_types:primitive_types.adoc#_boolean_class[Boolean]` [1..1]
a|String is a valid ISO 8601 date, i.e. takes the form:

* `hh:mm:ss[(,&#124;.)s+][Z&#124;±hh[:mm]]` (extended)
* `hhmmss[(,&#124;.)s+][Z&#124;±hh[mm]]` (compact)
* or one of the partial forms:
** `hh:mm` (extended)
** `hhmm` or `hh` (compact)

with an additional optional timezone indicator of:

* `Z` or `±hh[:mm]` (extended)  `±hh[mm]` (compact)

Where:

* `hh` is "00" - "23" (0-filled to two digits)
* `mm` is "00" - "59" (0-filled to two digits)
* `ss` is "00" - "60" (0-filled to two digits)
* `[(,&#124;.)s+]` is an optional string consisting of a comma or decimal point followed by numeric string of 1 or more digits, representing a fractional second
* `Z` is a literal meaning UTC (modern replacement for GMT), i.e. timezone `+0000`

): `xref:BASE:foundation_types:time_types.adoc#_iso8601_date_class[Iso8601_date]` [1..1]
a|Addition of nominal duration represented by `_a_diff_`. For example, a duration of `'P1Y'` means advance to the same date next year, with the exception of the date 29 February in a leap year, to which the addition of a nominal year will result in 28 February of the following year. Similarly, `'P1M'` is understood here as a nominal month, the addition of which will result in one of:

* the same day in the following month, if it exists, or;
* one or two days less where the following month is shorter, or;
* in the case of adding a month to the date 31 Jan, the result will be 28 Feb in a non-leap year (i.e. three less) and 29 Feb in a leap year (i.e. two less).
